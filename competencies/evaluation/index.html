Evaluation

April 26, 2026 · 2 min · Linda Jing

1. What was the design of your user study to evaluate your artifact?#

We conducted a task-based user study using our functional MVP, where students interacted with the planner interface directly. We asked 2 students to perform realistic tasks:

  • Adding courses to semesters
  • Checking whether they had completed major requirements
  • Using the auto-fill feature to generate a plan

We asked them to think aloud while completing these tasks and explain how they interpreted the requirement status updates (“Completed” vs. “Not completed”). We also asked follow-up questions about whether the system helped them understand their progress, how confident they felt in their plan, and what parts were confusing.

2. What were the results of this user study?#

Students responded positively to the dynamic requirement tracking feature. One student said it was “really helpful to see things turn green automatically,” because it clearly showed progress without needing to calculate manually. Another mentioned that “seeing which classes are used for each requirement makes it feel more real,” showing the usefulness of the “Used:” labels.

The semester layout also worked well. Students found it easy to add courses and visually organize their plan. One participant noted that “this is way better than planning in my head because I can actually see all 8 semesters.”

However, several issues remained. Students were still confused about requirements that were not completed, especially categories like Math Foundations, with one user saying “I don’t know what I should take for this.” Others pointed out that while the system shows what is completed, it does not guide them on what to take next. Additionally, users were unsure about prerequisites and course availability.

3. What were the main points of future development that you took away from this activity?#

From these results, we identified several key improvements for future development:

  • Clearer guidance on unmet requirements: suggesting specific courses that satisfy them.
  • Prerequisite validation: adding warnings to prevent unrealistic plans.
  • Enrollment signals: incorporating course availability information to address uncertainty.
  • Decision support: evolving the system to help users choose what to take next, not just track what is done.
