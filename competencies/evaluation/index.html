Evaluation

April 26, 2026 · 2 min · Linda Jing

Citation: This artifact for evaluation is ChatGPT generated based on our hand-drawn prototype on the blackboard in the Prototype phase.

1. What was the design of your user study to evaluate your artifact?#

We conducted a walkthrough-style user study using our refined prototype, where students were asked to simulate planning their four-year schedule. Participants were given three tasks:

  1. Checking their progress toward graduation
  2. Selecting courses for a semester
  3. Organizing courses across fall and spring terms

During the session, we asked them to speak and explain how they interpreted different parts of the interface, especially the requirement summary and course layout. We also asked questions while they were completing the tasks — such as how they track requirements, how they compare courses across semesters, and whether the interface reduced confusion.

2. What were the results of this user study?#

The 2 students generally found the structure intuitive and appreciated having requirements and credits in one place. One participant mentioned that it was “nice to see everything together instead of keeping it in my head,” showing that the integrated tracking reduced mental effort.

The fall/spring layout also helped users organize their plans, with one student noting that it “makes it easier to see if one semester is too heavy.”

However, several confusion points remained. Students said it was “not clear which exact courses fulfill requirements” and that they were “not sure if a class actually counts,” indicating that requirement visibility was still too high-level.

3. What were the main points of future development that you took away from this activity?#

From these results, we identified several key directions for improvement:

  • Detailed requirement mapping: clearly showing which courses fulfill which requirements.
  • Prerequisite visualization: adding warnings to help users understand course dependencies.
  • Better decision support: allowing users to compare alternative course options side by side.
  • Enrollment risk signals: incorporating information about course availability and enrollment risk to address uncertainty, which was one of the biggest pain points we learned from students.
